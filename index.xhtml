JIDs for Messaging with Phones

JMP gives you a real phone number for sending picture and text messages right from your existing XMPP account.

(If you are unfamiliar with XMPP, you can learn more here. Or, to be notified when the JMP app is ready to use, add your email address to our list.)

Frequently Asked Questions

Q1. What do I get when I signup for JMP?

JMP gives you a Canadian or US phone number that is yours to keep. JMP allows you to send and receive text messages and picture messages using your XMPP client, as well as to receive voice calls, which are forwarded to your existing Canadian or US phone number.

Some examples use cases include: texting friends in another country (ie. the US if you are in Canada) who can then reply without paying international texting fees, or chatting with friends over SMS using a real phone number even if you don't have or want a cell phone.

Q2. What do I need in order to signup for and use JMP?

Signing up for JMP requires only the following:

  • a federated XMPP account (an easy way to get one is with Conversations)
  • a Canadian or US phone number (can be voice-only, no Touch-Tone needed); support for using a SIP URI instead is in the works
  • payment details (see below)
Q3. How much does JMP cost?

JMP is currently in beta and the introductory rate for beta users is US$2.99 per month. This includes unlimited SMS and MMS, as well as 50 minutes of incoming voice calls.

You may cancel your subscription at any time (via PayPal or by contacting the support team). If you have chosen not to port your JMP number to a different carrier, your JMP number will be reclaimed after 30 days.

Once the beta period for JMP is over, the US$2.99 subscription will still be available and will still have unlimited incoming SMS and MMS, but the number of outgoing SMS/MMS will be limited; other plans will be available.

The beta period will last until at least April 2017, and a new unlimited messaging plan will be made available once the beta period has ended. After the beta period, additional non-PayPal methods of payment will be made available.

Q4. How do I get help with JMP or learn more about it?

The best venue for help using or developing features for JMP is the JMP/Soprani.ca multi-user chat (MUC, or group chat) room at discuss@conference.soprani.ca , accessible from most XMPP clients. Your questions can be answered most quickly there as several JMP users and developers are likely to be present at any given time.

If you'd like to send a private inquiry instead, or if the MUC does not work for you for some reason, please send a text message to the support team at +1 416 993 8000 (Canada) or +1 312 796 8000 (US) and we will get back to you within 24 hours. Both numbers can be texted from most other countries, though your carrier may charge an extra fee for international text messaging.

All of the software that makes up JMP is free and open source software. You can view, download, and modify the source code here.

For news about JMP, you can signup for our low-volume notification list here.

Q5. Which text messaging features are supported?

JMP supports the following text messaging features:

  • Send and receive text and picture messages (SMS and MMS) between your JMP number and any other Canadian or US phone number.
  • Send and receive text and picture messages with most Canadian and US short codes. For example, you can send "info" to 33733 and receive the reply.
  • Use any emoji or other Unicode character in your text messages.
  • Delivery receipts, as indicated by the carrier. To receive these for a given contact, they must be in your contact list (roster).
  • Receive group text messages (the list of recipients will be available in a future release).
  • Receive text messages from most countries in the world.

JMP does not (yet) support these features:

  • Send group text messages.
  • Send text messages to countries outside the US and Canada.
Q6. How do I send text messages?

Text messages are sent and receive using special Jabber IDs (JIDs). To send a text message, first add the JID representing the destination phone number to your roster. For example, to send a text message to +1 416 993 8000 you would add "+14169938000@catapult.sgx.soprani.ca" to your roster. You can then send the contact a message or picture and they will receive it as an SMS/MMS message.

To send text messages to short codes, use the special suffix for short codes, ie. "33733;phone-context=ca-us.phone-context.soprani.ca@catapult.sgx.soprani.ca" represents the 33733 short code. This will be simplified in the future, but is required for now in order to maintain proper uniqueness going forward.

Q7. Do I have to use PayPal to signup for JMP?

No. Other payment methods are available. For details, please contact our support team by sending a text message to +1 416 993 8000 (Canada) or +1 312 796 8000 (US) or send a private message to "ossguy" in the JMP/Soprani.ca MUC at discuss@conference.soprani.ca .

Q8. Can I port my existing number into JMP?

Yes, but the process is not currently automated, and may take 1-2 weeks, depending on the number being ported in. For details, please contact our support team by sending a text message to +1 416 993 8000 (Canada) or +1 312 796 8000 (US) or send a private message to "ossguy" in the JMP/Soprani.ca MUC at discuss@conference.soprani.ca .

Q9. Which XMPP clients are supported?

You can use any XMPP client you like. If a client doesn't work for some reason, please file a ticket or discuss it with the team. JMP is normally tested with Gajim and Conversations, since they both support the needed XEPs for JMP's complete feature set (which includes XEP-0184, XEP-0234, and XEP-0261, among others).
